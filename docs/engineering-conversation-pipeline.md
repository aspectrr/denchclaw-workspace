# Engineering Conversation Pipeline

## The Two Tracks

- Engineering conversations = Learning about the work, building peer relationships, finding pain points
- VP/Team Lead conversations = Understanding budget, headcount constraints, selling the "AI employee" concept

Both are valuable. The engineering track feeds into the VP track via introductions.

---

## Engineering Pipeline (Bottom-Up)

### Stage 1: Not Connected

- Find engineers via LinkedIn search or snowball method
- Send blank connection request
- Action: Wait for acceptance

### Stage 2: Connected to Opener (Text)

- Connection accepted
- Send first message:
"Hey [Name], I'm an ELK engineer at OmniSOC. Noticed you're working with Elastic SIEM. Curious what your stack looks like day-to-day?"
- Goal: Get a response, confirm they do ELK work

### Stage 3: Opener to Chat (Text)

- Brief back-and-forth on LinkedIn messaging
- Ask 2-3 questions:
  - What SIEM are you running?
  - On-prem or cloud?
  - How big is your team?
- Goal: Confirm they are a peer doing similar work, build basic rapport

### Stage 4: Chat to Meeting Ask (THIS IS WHERE MEETINGS FIT)

After 3-5 messages of good back-and-forth, suggest a call.

Transition message:  
"Man, this sounds really similar to what I deal with. Would you be down for a quick 15-20 min call? I'd love to hear more about how your team handles things. My work is pretty busy day to day."

Why this works:

- You already established you are a peer (not a salesperson)
- "Swapping war stories" is low-pressure, engineer-friendly framing
- 15-20 min is a small ask
- "No agenda" removes the sales concern

### Stage 5: The Meeting (15-20 min, video or phone)

Meeting structure (loose, not scripted):

1. Min 0-5: Introductions, what you both do, team size
2. Min 5-10: "Walk me through your stack" - let them explain their setup
3. Min 10-15: "What takes up most of your time?" - pain point discovery
4. Min 15-20: "If you could automate one thing..." - future positioning

What you are listening for:

- Pain points that match your experience (DLQs, Kafka lag, pipeline debugging)
- How much time they spend on manual work
- Whether they feel understaffed
- Tools they have tried and why they did not work

After the meeting:

- Thank them
- Add notes to CRM (pain points, stack details, team size)
- If their company passes the qualification checklist, find their team lead

### Stage 6: Bridge to VP/Team Lead

If the engineer is at a qualified company:

- Ask: "Who manages your team? I'd love to learn how leadership thinks about tooling"
- Or find their manager via LinkedIn sidebar / company page
- Reference the engineer conversation when reaching out to the VP
- This is how bottom-up becomes a sale

## VP/Team Lead Pipeline (Top-Down)

This runs in parallel. You do not need an engineer intro to talk to VPs.

### Stage 1: Not Connected

- Find VPs/Directors at qualified companies
- Send blank connection request

### Stage 2: Connected to 3-Part Opener

- Use the 3-part LinkedIn messaging sequence from MEMORY.md
- Message 1: "I'm an ELK engineer at OmniSOC and noticed you're at [Company]. Curious what SIEM stack you're running day-to-day?"
- Message 2: "I'm trying to learn from folks further up in industry about how different teams handle SIEMs, specifically on-prem setups and team sizing."
- Message 3: "Totally understand if you're busy, but if you're open to a quick chat, I'd love to hear about your stack and what's working (or not working) for your team."

### Stage 3: VP Meeting (20-30 min)

- Focus on: headcount, budget constraints, team pain points
- Use VP questions from /docs/first-10-conversations-plan.md
- Position as "AI employee" not "developer tool"

---

## How the Two Tracks Connect

BOTTOM-UP (Engineering)          TOP-DOWN (VP/Team Lead)
Find ELK engineer                Find VP at qualified company
        |                                |
Connect + text chat               Connect + 3-part message
        |                                |
Meeting (learn their setup)       VP Meeting (learn budget/pain)
        |                                |
Find their team lead              Same VP
        |
Intro to team lead ----------> Now in VP pipeline

The tracks converge when:

1. Bottom-up finds the VP (engineer introduces you to their manager)
2. Top-down finds the engineer (you reach VP directly, they point you to their team)
